# Slate Platform Demo Script

**Presenter:** Mike Madden
**Audience:** School Finance Consultants & Network Leaders
**Duration:** 25-30 minutes
**Setup:** Open the URL from a logged-out state so the splash runs on entry.

---

## 1. The Entrance (Splash Screen)

**Action:** Open the Slate URL. Let the subtle aperture animation run without clicking.

What you are looking at is not a dashboard. It is an intelligence platform built specifically for the complexities of running a school network. We call it Slate. The philosophy is simple: start with the facts. In my 17 years at Noble, I realized we didn't need more data warehouses or operational systems. We needed a lens to see the signal through the noise. Slate sits above your existing systems — financial, student information, HR, operations — and synthesizes it into actionable intelligence for the CEO and CFO.

**Action:** Click "I Understand — Enter Slate."

---

## 2. Dashboard (The Command Center)

**Action:** Land on Dashboard. Pause. Let the design register.

This is the morning briefing. It gives a network leader everything they need to know in 60 seconds. Notice the design: a dark slate shell for navigation, a warm canvas, and crisp white cards. It is built to reduce cognitive load. You are looking at the data, not the interface.

The AI Intelligence Briefing at the top synthesizes the most critical updates across the network. Below that, the Vital Signs: enrollment is at 6,823, exactly on target. YTD surplus is $5.3M with a DSCR of 3.47x. We have 215 days of cash on hand. We are tracking 3 Tier 1 risks. In one glance, I know the network is financially sound, enrollment is holding, and I know exactly where to focus my attention today.

---

## 3. Intelligence: Briefing

**Action:** Click Briefing in the sidebar.

This is the executive intelligence briefing. It pulls from every module in Slate to create a single, narrative-driven report for the CEO.

We see the financial position is strong. Enrollment is meeting targets. But under Risk Register, we see 3 Tier 1 risks active, and one is trending upward — labor relations. The compliance briefing shows we have a state reporting deadline in 14 days. The civic briefing flags that Senate Bill 2907, a positive per-pupil funding equity adjustment, just passed committee. This is the synthesis of a dozen different operational systems into one readable page.

---

## 4. Intelligence: Watch (Real-Time Safety)

**Action:** Click Watch in the sidebar.

When you run schools in a major city, safety is not a static data point. It is dynamic. This is Watch. It is a real-time safety intelligence module that filters out the noise and tracks only what matters: shootings, homicides, weapons incidents, and shots fired within a critical radius of our 10 campuses.

Right now, the network status is CLEAR. All 10 campuses are green. If an incident occurs near Englewood or Woodlawn, the status shifts to AMBER or RED, the campus card pulses, and the AI Briefing updates instantly with context, including whether a dismissal window is active. This is about knowing what happens as it happens, so principals can make informed decisions in real time rather than finding out on the evening news.

---

## 5. Intelligence: Signal (Existential Threat Radar)

**Action:** Click Signal in the sidebar.

Watch tracks what is happening right now. Signal tracks what is coming. Signal is our existential threat intelligence engine. It operates like a geopolitical risk analyst at a sovereign wealth fund, scanning multiple layers of the environment to detect converging patterns that precede institutional decline.

The Threat Radar shows us the interconnected web of risks. The Horizon Scan projects these threats forward. It identifies when a seemingly minor shift in authorizer sentiment, combined with a subtle demographic trend, creates a massive structural risk three years from now.

---

## 6. Operations: Ledger (Financial Intelligence Engine)

**Action:** Click Ledger. Default view is the Overview tab.

This is the financial engine. We rebuilt Ledger to function like the intelligence systems used by elite financial firms, not like a school budget spreadsheet. The layout is deliberate. At the top, cash posture and budget health. We are running a $138.3M annual budget. YTD revenue is $80.8M against an $78.5M budget. YTD expenses are $75.5M against a $74.2M budget. The AI Financial Overview below provides a narrative analysis of what those numbers mean, not just what they are.

**Action:** Click the Covenants tab.

For a CFO, bond covenants are existential. Instead of burying these in a spreadsheet, we brought them to the surface. Five covenants, five cards, all green. DSCR is 3.47x, a 247% cushion above the 1.0x minimum. Days Cash is 215. Current Ratio is 3.00x. Net Asset Ratio is 69.1%. Minimum Enrollment is 6,823. Every single one is compliant.

**Action:** Click the Scenarios tab.

This is where Ledger becomes a strategic tool. What happens if per-pupil funding drops 2% next year? What if we increase staff compensation by 5% to stay competitive? As I move these sliders, the 5-year projection table updates in real time — EBITDA, Days Cash, and DSCR through FY30. This is the conversation a CFO and CEO should be having before budget season, not during it.

**Action:** Click the Board Deck tab.

When it is time for the finance committee meeting, you don't spend three days building slides. Slate takes the current financial reality, the covenant compliance, and the scenario models, and generates a narrative-driven board deck with one click.

---

## 7. Operations: Scholar (Enrollment & Pipeline)

**Action:** Click Scholar in the sidebar.

Scholar is our enrollment and demographic intelligence module. Enrollment is the lifeblood of a charter network.

We are currently at 6,823 students, 100% of our target. But Scholar doesn't just show the current number; it tracks the pipeline. It shows us application velocity, historical yield rates, and attrition trends. We can see exactly which campuses are running a waitlist and which are fighting for every seat, allowing us to deploy marketing resources surgically.

---

## 8. Operations: Grounds (Facilities & Capital)

**Action:** Click Grounds in the sidebar.

Facilities are your largest physical asset and often your largest hidden liability. Grounds tracks the physical health of the network.

Our network Facility Condition Index (FCI) is 70, which is fair. We are tracking 12 active work orders and 5 major capital projects. The Chatham Roof Replacement and the Austin HVAC System Replacement are critical projects. Grounds gives us visibility into deferred maintenance backlogs before they become emergency capital crises.

---

## 9. Strategy: Shield (Risk Management)

**Action:** Click Shield in the sidebar.

A network leader is ultimately a risk manager. Shield is our enterprise risk register. We are currently tracking 3 Tier 1 risks that require board-level attention.

Charter Renewal Risk: the next renewal cycle is 2027. We are actively building the evidence portfolio now.
Labor Relations and Workforce Stability: this is trending upward. We are monitoring staff turnover closely.
Tier 3/4 Student Behaviors: post-pandemic behavioral trends are persistent. We are expanding counseling capacity.

For each risk, Slate tracks the likelihood, impact, velocity, and the specific mitigation status. Nothing falls through the cracks.

---

## 10. Strategy: Fund (Philanthropic Pipeline)

**Action:** Click Fund in the sidebar.

We have to resource the mission. Fund tracks our philanthropic and competitive grant pipeline. We have $5.8M in the active pipeline across 8 opportunities.

The Chicago Community Trust negotiation is at 80% probability. The Walton Family Foundation cultivation at $1M is at 35%. This gives the CEO and Development team a clear, weighted view of future philanthropic revenue so there are no surprises at year-end.

---

## 11. Strategy: Civic (Legislative Intelligence)

**Action:** Click Civic in the sidebar.

Charter schools exist in a highly politicized environment. Civic tracks the legislative and regulatory landscape.

We are currently tracking three key bills. House Bill 4821 would modify the charter renewal process — this is a WATCH. Senate Bill 2907 is a per-pupil funding equity adjustment — this is POSITIVE. House Bill 5102 expands safety reporting requirements. Civic maps these bills against our key stakeholders and authorizer relationships, allowing us to mobilize advocacy efforts precisely when and where they matter.

---

**Action:** Return to the Dashboard.

That is Slate. It is not about having more data. It is about having the right intelligence, synthesized beautifully, so leaders can make the decisions that matter. This is what I wish I had had for the last 17 years.
